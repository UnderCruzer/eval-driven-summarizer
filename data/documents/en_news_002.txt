Elon Musk's xAI sues Colorado over new rules for artificial intelligence

Musk's AI company challenged Colorado's comprehensive AI regulation law, arguing it restricts free speech protections. The lawsuit contends the legislation would require the company to "promote the state's ideological views on various matters, racial justice in particular". Colorado became the first state to pass broad AI regulatory measures designed to prevent algorithmic discrimination across education, employment, and financial services sectors.

The legal challenge, filed in federal court, argues that the Colorado AI Act violates the First Amendment by compelling xAI to alter its models' outputs to conform to state-mandated standards. xAI's lawyers argued that the law effectively forces the company to "speak" in ways it does not choose, which they claim constitutes unconstitutional government compulsion of speech.

Colorado's governor signed the AI Act into law earlier this year after a contentious legislative session. The law requires developers of high-risk AI systems to conduct impact assessments, disclose when AI is used in consequential decisions, and provide users with ways to appeal AI-generated outcomes in areas such as hiring, lending, and school admissions.

Supporters of the law argue that algorithmic discrimination is a real and documented harm, and that the legislation is a necessary consumer protection measure. Civil rights groups have submitted briefs supporting Colorado's right to regulate discriminatory AI systems.

Legal experts are watching the case closely, as it could set a precedent for whether AI companies can use First Amendment arguments to shield their models from anti-discrimination requirements. Similar laws are under consideration in more than a dozen other US states.
